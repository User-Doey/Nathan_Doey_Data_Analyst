Tableau Interactive Visuals and IFSC Climbing

Paris 2024 Olympics are coming soon, this is the second time climbing is part of the competition. To understand who to look out for and which countries are hopeful for medals we can look at the past year of competitive climbing. In another piece we used SQL to create a dataset exploring the 2023 IFSC (International Federation of Sport Climbing) bouldering results, to give an insight into what the scores could look like at the Olympics. This Tableau dashboard continues this exploration and uses interactive visuals to explore four questions.

1. Which athlete had the best results?
We can see the number of golds, medals and finals of the top athletes.

2. Which athlete climbed the best?
Not all athletes compete across a whole season, so looking at average Tops can show who climbed best (If a climber completes a climb they are awarded a ‘Top’, the most import metric in scoring).

3. Which country had the best results?
The map shows how many athletes represent each country and their cumulative results.

4. Which was the hardest competition?
The fewer Tops in a competition, the harder the climbs.
